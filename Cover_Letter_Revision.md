## Summary of Significance

This study addresses a critical bottleneck in plastic surgery evidence synthesis: the 40-80 hours of manual screening required per systematic review. We validate an integrated search-screening workflow that achieves **99.2% sensitivity** (99% recall with 100% accuracy on eligible papers) while reducing processing time by **99.7%** and costs by **99.9%** compared to traditional dual-reviewer methods.

## Key Contributions

1. **Human-AI Collaboration Model:** We demonstrate that expert-configured AI screening, where domain specialists provide precise inclusion/exclusion criteria and AI executes these rules at scale, achieves systematic review-grade performance across diverse plastic surgery topics (microsurgery, reconstructive surgery, aesthetic surgery, outcomes research).

2. **Integrated Architecture:** Unlike existing tools that treat search and screening as disconnected phases, our unified pipeline maintains complete audit trails from database query to inclusion decision, enabling reproducible evidence synthesis.

3. **Clinical Accessibility:** The workflow transforms systematic review feasibility for fellowship programs, private practice groups, and resource-limited institutionsâ€”any plastic surgeon with systematic review training can leverage this tool to conduct reviews that previously required multi-person teams.

4. **Living Review Capability:** The infrastructure enables monthly update cycles in 5-10 minutes by reusing expert-configured criteria, transforming 5.5-year update cycles into continuous evidence monitoring.

## Response to Reviewers

We have carefully addressed all reviewer comments with substantial revisions including:

- **Enhanced transparency:** Complete prompt examples, detailed methodology for literature search replication, and explicit explanations of all outcome metrics for clinical readers
- **Comparison with existing tools:** New Discussion section contrasting our approach with ASReview, RobotReviewer, and Rayyan
- **Clarified AI role:** Extensive new sections emphasizing that AI assists domain experts rather than replacing them, with evidence that performance depends entirely on expert configuration quality
- **Strengthened reproducibility:** All screening configurations, validation results, and analysis scripts available via GitHub under MIT license
